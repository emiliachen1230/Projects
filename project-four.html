Selected work / 2022

Rondo Door Bell
Interaction Device

Product x PCB

Rondo rethinks the door bell as an interactive musical object. It transforms the act of arrival into an event of sound, contact, and atmosphere, turning a functional signal into a relational interface.

Keywords

Arduino, Bluetooth, sound interaction, object interface, prototype.

Material

Ebony, brass, plastic, PCB, fabricated components.

Technical

Programming, embedded interaction, circuit board design, prototyping, CNC-based fabrication.

Overview

A more composed and exhibition-like project page, designed to feel aligned with the homepage rather than like a generic placeholder.

The project places music at the threshold of space. Instead of treating sound as decoration or secondary feedback, it becomes the central carrier of welcome, presence, and emotion. By combining key-based interaction with sensing and audio output, Rondo produces a small but distinct shift in everyday ritual.

Formally, the object borrows from the visual language of brass instruments. Technically, it brings together electronics, material construction, and interface thinking. The result is not simply a product prototype, but a device that stages sound as contact.

Interaction

Users trigger sound through touch and entry. The device responds with tonal output rather than a neutral alert.

Form

The object references horn and wind-instrument morphology, giving the interface a recognizable acoustic identity.

System

Embedded programming, circuit design, and material fabrication are integrated into one coherent interaction object.
