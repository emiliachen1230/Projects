Soultime

Soultime is a service-oriented mobile app developed from research on aging society, time banking, and age-friendly interface design. The project translates long-term care needs, social participation, and digital usability into a dual-platform system for elderly users and family members, combining service exchange, activity participation, health records, and family coordination in one visual and interaction framework.

Service Design Aging Society Time Bank UI / UX Brand Identity

Project Overview

Soultime begins from a clear gap: traditional care systems are limited by workforce shortage, while many existing digital products still do not fully respond to elderly users' social participation, service access, and interface comprehension. The project therefore treats time banking not only as a service model, but as a design framework for building mutual support, digital inclusion, and practical daily care.

The final outcome is not just one interface. It includes a complete design translation process from literature review, case study comparison, and user interviews to brand identity, app information architecture, user flow, wireframe development, visual system, and exhibition presentation. The project is structured around Soultime for elderly users and Soultime Family for family-side support and monitoring.

Core Problems

01

Care Workforce Shortage

Long-term care systems still rely heavily on professional labor and passive support, which limits flexibility and access in daily care scenarios.

02

Digital Adaptation Gap

Middle-aged and elderly users often face difficulty with layered navigation, low feedback clarity, and interfaces that are not aligned with their cognitive habits.

03

Weak Social Participation

Many existing elderly care apps emphasize monitoring or medical support, but rarely provide meaningful systems for contribution, exchange, and social connection.

Research Foundation

Aging Society

The research reviews the challenges brought by aging society, including care demand expansion, family burden, limited social participation, and the need for accessible digital services.

Time Banking

Time banking is examined as a mutual support model where service hours become an exchange unit, creating both social value and practical care support.

Age-Friendly Interface

International and applied interface principles are consolidated into design criteria including layout hierarchy, iconography, typography, color perception, consistency, and accessibility.

Through literature analysis, case comparison, and qualitative interviews, the project confirms that a time-bank-based service app must respond not only to function, but also to readability, emotional reassurance, and actual service flow.

Methodology

Stage 01

Literature Analysis

Reviewed aging society, time bank models, and age-friendly interface guidelines to define the service gap and establish design criteria.

Stage 02

Case Analysis

Compared Handy Guardian, Yodda Enable, and GrandPad to identify differences in service positioning, UI structure, interaction patterns, and visual strategy.

Stage 03

Qualitative Interviews

Interviewed 15 middle-aged and elderly participants aged 55–75 to collect smartphone habits, interface preferences, opinions on time banking, and desired app functions.

Stage 04

Design Translation

Converted research findings into persona, journey map, feature strategy, brand identity, site map, wireframe, and visual interface design.

Interview Insights

Sample

User Profile

15 interviewees in Northern Taiwan, aged 55 to 75. More than half had not previously participated in community service activities, but all had smartphone usage experience.

Usage Pattern

Digital Habits

Participants used smartphones for an average of 3.75 hours per day, mainly for news, articles, communication, and social media, indicating familiarity with simple mobile operations.

Need

Priority Functions

Users emphasized operation guidance, navigation to service locations, calendar reminders, trust-building mechanisms, and easy access to service records.

Design Criteria from Research

Layout Hierarchy

Few page levels, clear function divisions, generous white space, and full-screen content grouping to reduce cognitive load.

Iconography

Concrete icons with text labels, consistent visual style, direct function reflection, and avoidance of overly abstract or complex symbols.

Typography

Clear solid type, large type size around 20pt, strong contrast with background, simple wording, and the option to adjust text size.

Color Perception

Bright, low-saturation colors with clear contrast. The visual system should remain readable, calm, and not overload one screen with too many accents.

Style Consistency

Flat visual language combined with subtle neumorphism or shadow cues where needed, maintaining coherence across buttons, cards, and image assets.

Functional Accessibility

Large tap targets, rounded buttons, minimal text input burden, direct CTA patterns, and support through voice, captions, and feedback prompts.

Design Strategy

The project transforms research findings into three design directions. First, it reconstructs time banking into an understandable service system through service application, invitation, confirmation, hour accumulation, and review feedback. Second, it creates a dual-platform structure that addresses both elderly user participation and family-side coordination. Third, it redefines elderly-friendly interface design as a practical combination of guidance, reassurance, readability, and controllable interaction.

This means Soultime is not positioned as a medical app or only a scheduling tool. It is designed as a mutual-support service platform that combines care access, participation incentives, and a visual language soft enough for daily use. The interface therefore has to feel clear, familiar, and stable, while still supporting rich service scenarios and records.

A

Service Exchange

Translate time currency into understandable service records, hour accumulation, and exchange flow.

B

Family Coordination

Build a parallel family-side interface for activity tracking, health updates, and communication support.

C

Friendly Interaction

Reduce operation burden through guided flows, large touch targets, and low-complexity information architecture.

Brand Positioning

Soultime is positioned as a caring, age-friendly, and service-oriented digital platform. The brand does not frame elderly users as only recipients of care, but as participants who can request support, contribute time, join activities, and remain socially connected. The identity therefore balances gentleness and trust, while keeping enough functional clarity for service-based tasks.

The project also develops Soultime Family as a linked version for family members. This extension keeps the same identity system while adjusting information emphasis toward monitoring, communication, reminders, and reassurance. Together, both versions build a coordinated ecosystem rather than two disconnected interfaces.

CIS Overview

Visual Identity

Aa
Typeface & Tone

The typographic system prioritizes readability and calm rhythm. Display typography supports brand character, while the body type remains highly readable and stable for long-term screen use. The hierarchy is restrained and avoids crowded, technical-looking layouts.

Emergency #EC776B

Interview results showed that many participants associated health-related applications with blue-green color families. Instead of directly applying a stronger blue-green palette, the final design shifts toward a softer light-blue main tone. This keeps the expected sense of trust and health-related clarity, while reducing the colder or overly clinical feeling that can appear in saturated blue-green interfaces. The visual result is more harmonious, approachable, and suitable for repeated everyday use.

Information Architecture

Soultime

The elderly-user version is structured around service application, invitations, confirmed orders, center activities, navigation, chat, records, badges, health status, and notifications. The homepage acts as the first layer, while key tasks are guided through shallow hierarchy and return-friendly flow.

Soultime Family

The family-side version focuses on viewing elderly status, activity participation, health summaries, location-related support, and direct communication. It avoids over-control and instead prioritizes reassurance, visibility, and contactability.

Persona & Journey

Persona

Mr. A

65-year-old retired user with regular community activity participation and basic smartphone familiarity. His primary goals are to access service information quickly, complete service application with low friction, and receive reminders without confusion.

Journey Mapping

The journey is divided into service application, invitation check, order confirmation, route navigation, activity participation, and service record review. Pain points appear mainly at first-time operation, route uncertainty, and process comprehension. These findings directly informed guidance design, reminder placement, and navigation integration.

App Interface

Home · Request · Activities

The main interface emphasizes large readable content blocks, shallow task depth, and immediate recognition of key actions. Left and right horizontal access extend the home page toward navigation and chat, reducing menu burden.

Records · Health · Profile

Supporting pages integrate service records, health summaries, and account settings without breaking visual consistency. Icons and CTA positions remain stable across screens to support memory and repeated use.

Soultime App Functions

01

Login & Onboarding

Supports registration and account entry, followed by a simple health confirmation and visual onboarding sequence to strengthen recognition and emotional tone.

02

Homepage Hub

Provides direct access to request help, help others, calendar, emergency call, profile, navigation, and chat from the first layer of the system.

03

Request Help

Allows users to create new service requests, check progress, receive approval notifications, and add confirmed support to the schedule.

04

Help Others

Displays nearby support needs and enables users to join community assistance through application and matching flow.

05

Calendar & Reminders

Shows confirmed orders, activities, and reminders through year, month, and day views, supporting predictable time planning.

06

Badge System

Visualizes service expert, helping hand, and high-rating achievements to strengthen participation motivation and visible contribution.

07

Time Currency Records

Tracks service hours, transfer records, gifts, check-in, check-out, and redemption history as part of the time banking mechanism.

08

Navigation

Supports route guidance to activity and service locations, reducing uncertainty and external dependence during travel.

09

Health Status Record

Displays the latest record and historical summaries of mental, physical, and emotional conditions through simple chart-based feedback.

10

Chat & Family Contact

Creates direct communication between users and family or service-related contacts, supporting reassurance and coordination.

Soultime Family Functions

01

Status Overview

Allows family members to quickly review the elderly user’s latest health entry, recent activity participation, and service status.

02

Location Support

Links the home view to map and location-related guidance so that family members can understand route context without directly interfering in activity flow.

03

Communication Window

Connects directly to chat so that reminders, check-ins, and emotional support can happen through a familiar conversation pattern.

04

Monitoring with Restraint

The family-side design is intended to reassure rather than over-control, keeping the elderly user’s autonomy visible within the system.

Soultime for Family

Family Version Prototype

Design Intention

This version is designed for family members who participate in daily care, emotional support, and long-term companionship. It expands Soultime from a personal interface into a relational care platform.

Key Difference

While the original Soultime focuses on the individual user, the family version emphasizes connection, observation, and shared participation, allowing care to become a visible and collaborative experience.

Compared with the original version, Soultime for Family shifts the focus from personal reflection to shared awareness. The interface is intended to help family members check emotional status, understand daily conditions, and participate in care routines through a softer and more approachable digital experience.

Design Contributions

Time Bank Translation

The project translates the abstract concept of time banking into understandable service records, exchange flow, and reward logic for elderly users.

Dual-Platform Service Logic

By developing both Soultime and Soultime Family, the project addresses not only elderly use but also family-side reassurance and coordination.

Research-Based UI System

The visual and interaction rules are directly grounded in literature, case analysis, and interviews rather than purely stylistic preference.

Outcome & Reflection

Soultime confirms that the combination of time banking logic and age-friendly interface design can respond to both service access and social participation. The project does not simply create a care app, but proposes an alternative digital environment where elderly users can request help, contribute, connect, and remain visible within a mutual support network.

As a project outcome, the app also establishes a visual and structural foundation that can be extended into future testing, service collaboration, and exhibition display. The prototype, brand identity, and interface system together form a coherent design proposal grounded in both research and practice.
